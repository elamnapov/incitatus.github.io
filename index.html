About the Empire

The Roman Empire (27 BC – AD 476 in the West) was one of the greatest civilizations in human history. It spanned continents, languages, and cultures, leaving behind revolutionary architecture, law, and literature.

Highlights

The Colosseum

Built AD 80, it hosted gladiator contests, public spectacles, and remains one of Rome’s iconic monuments.

Territory Map

At its peak the empire extended from Britain in the northwest to Egypt in the southeast.

Engineering & Trade

Rome excelled in road networks, aqueducts, marketplaces, and architectural innovation.

Legacy

Roman law, language, governance, and infrastructure still influence modern societies. The Latin language evolved into the Romance languages, and many legal systems borrow concepts from Roman jurisprudence.
